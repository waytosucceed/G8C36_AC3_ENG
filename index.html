Tenses : Simple Future and Future Continuous Tense

Simple future tense and future continuous tense both describe future events, but they emphasise different aspects of the action.

Simple future tense (e.g., "I will read the book") focuses on actions that will occur at a specific time in the future, often highlighting the fact that the action will happen.

In contrast, future continuous tense (e.g., "I will be reading the book") emphasises the ongoing nature of an action at a particular future moment, indicating that the action will be in progress.

While the simple future tense describes what will happen, the future continuous tense describes what will be happening at that future time.

For example: "She will cook dinner at 7 PM" (simple future) indicates the action of cooking will occur, whereas
"She will be cooking dinner at 7 PM" (future continuous) suggests that cooking will be actively happening at that specific time.

Words that I Learnt from the Story

Intervene:
Meaning: To come between or alter the course of events, often to prevent or modify something. It can also mean to interfere in a situation to help resolve a problem or improve a situation.
Example: The teacher decided to intervene when she saw the students arguing in the hallway.

Aligned:
Meaning: To be in a straight line or properly positioned; to be in agreement or harmony with something.
Example: Her personal values are closely aligned with the mission of the non-profit organisation.

Oblivion:
Meaning: The state of being unaware or unconscious of what is happening around one; complete forgetfulness or the state of being forgotten.
Example: After the scandal, the once-famous celebrity faded into oblivion.
